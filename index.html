The principle

Two reads and a stopwatch

The whole system is one idea done reliably: read the plate on the way in, read it again on the way out, subtract the two timestamps, and apply your free-period rule.

01

Arrive

Vehicle approaches. The unit is always watching; the IR illuminator handles night.

02

Read & log

ANPR extracts the plate and writes a timestamped entry record.

03

Park free

The free window runs (e.g. 30 min). The driver does nothing at all.

04

Leave

The exit read pairs to the entry by matching the same plate.

05

Decide

Within the window → no charge. Over it → a charge against the keeper.

How a site actually gets kitted out

Four mounting routes, cheapest effort first:

💡

Existing lamp post

Strap/clamp the unit to a post already at the entrance. No groundwork. Power from the column where allowed.

🧱

Wall mount

Bolt to a wall or gatepost beside the lane at plate height. Run a single PoE cable back indoors.

🚧

New short pole

Concrete a 1.2 m galvanised post beside the lane. Most controllable angle; small civils job.

📶

Solar + 4G

No mains/cabling? Battery + solar lid + 4G SIM makes it fully wireless for remote sites.

Bill of materials + stack

What you actually need to build it

Three layers: the camera unit at the kerb, the platform that pairs reads and bills, and the UK legal/data plumbing that makes a charge enforceable. ✓ verified = confirmed in the research; others are sensible build estimates.

📷 Camera unit (per lane)

  • 5MP global-shutter IR camera, 850nm, 0.1 Lux ≈ £120 ✓
  • Varifocal lens (frame the plate line) £20–60
  • Edge compute — Raspberry Pi 5 or Jetson £60–500
  • IP66/67 weather enclosure + mount £30–120
  • PoE / 12V power + 4G or Wi-Fi £20–80
  • Or buy an all-in-one ANPR camera Hikvision / Dahua

⚙️ Software platform

  • ANPR engine (read the plate) DIY / paid
  • Entry/exit pairing + overstay logic your code
  • Cloud backend + database SaaS
  • Operator dashboard + appeals portal web app
  • Payment + charge-notice workflow Stripe + post
  • Whitelist (staff/permits) & reporting config

⚖️ UK compliance

  • BPA or IPC accredited membership mandatory ✓
  • DVLA KADOE keeper lookup £2.50/req ✓
  • POFA 2012 compliant notices to charge keeper
  • ICO registration + UK GDPR / DPIA required
  • ICO surveillance / CCTV code required
  • Compliant entrance + tariff signage on site ✓
The camera is the easy 20%. In the UK you can't get a keeper's address from the DVLA, and can't reliably enforce a charge, unless you're a member of an Accredited Operator Scheme (BPA or IPC) and follow POFA 2012. The scheme membership, signage and data protection are the 80% that make this a real, sellable business.

UK rulebook

From "photo of a plate" to "enforceable charge"

Figures below are from official/industry sources; the verify pass got rate-limited on the legal claims, so treat them as well-sourced but worth a final solicitor check.

A

Join a scheme

Become a member of the BPA or IPC. Required before the DVLA will share keeper data. ✓

B

Get DVLA KADOE

Electronic keeper lookup — about £2.50 per request, charged even for "no trace". ✓

C

Follow POFA 2012

Serve the Notice to Keeper in the right window/format so the keeper is liable if the driver is unknown.

D

Protect the data

ICO registration, DPIA, surveillance code, minimal retention, a real appeals route.

Single Code of Practice (BPA/IPC): parking charges capped around £100, with a ~40% reduction for prompt payment, plus a mandatory grace period on top of a minimum consideration period before any charge bites. Build these limits into your platform from day one.
